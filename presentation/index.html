The PM job here isn't to optimize metrics. It's to understand what a woman in this journey actually needs — which is almost never what it looks like on the surface.

The non-obvious insight
Teleconsultation
Surface problem: 70% of non-converters weren't buying.

What most PMs would do: Hire fertility experts. Build a chat widget.

What we actually did: Went 25+ interviews deep into how fertility coaches and OBGYNs actually talk to women in this moment — the language they use, the fears they address, the sequence they follow.

Then built SOPs that replicated that environment. Not experts on call. A system that thinks and communicates like one.

The insight: women in this category don't need information. They need to feel like someone who understands their specific situation is guiding them.
The non-obvious insight
LTV / Churn
Surface problem: High churn after cycle 1 and cycle 2.

What most PMs would do: Push more strips. Discount. Remind users.

What we actually found: The inflection point in a woman's decision to continue using Inito isn't purchase. It isn't even usage. It's ovulation confirmation.

When she sees her hormone pattern and gets confirmation that she ovulated — she trusts the product. She continues. She doesn't churn.

But getting to ovulation confirmation depends entirely on her biological cycle — not her intent. Women were running out of strips before their body reached ovulation. No confirmation. No value. Churn.

The reframe: Strips aren't consumable inventory. They are the gating mechanism for value realization.

What we built:
· Checkout bundles sized to biological cycle length, not average purchase behavior
· Subscription redesigned around user psychology — cancel anytime, refund unused strips, flexible continuity not recurring obligation
